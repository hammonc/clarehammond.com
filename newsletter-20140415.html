May is a busy month for new music as Clare gives three world premieres including Velocity by Adam Gorb, Coniunctio by Andrew Keeling and the Five Songs of Emily Brontë by Robin Walker. At the beginning of the month she will perform Prospero's Isle by James Francis Brown with cellist and narrator Matthew Sharp as part of the RE:naissance festival at Kings Place. Designed to celebrate Shakespeare's legacy, you can find out more about this exciting festival by downloading a flyer here or listening to a podcast on Soundcloud. Clare will make her debut recitals at the Vale of Glamorgan and Beaumaris Festivals and returns to the Two Rivers Festival in the Wirral to perform with cellist Ken Woods, violinist Suzanne Casey, soprano Jane Wilkinson and narrator Peter Davison in a Brontë-themed programme.

Clare will celebrate the centenary of composer Andrzej Panufnik's birth this year with a disc of the complete solo piano works of Andrzej and Roxanna Panufnik for BIS Records. This will be released in the summer prior to a centenary tour of Poland, which will feature her debut recital at the Chopin and his Europe Festival and a recital and lecture at the Fryderyk Chopin University of Music in Warsaw. Clare is also co-curating a centenary festival at Kings Place in November where she will perform alongside the Brodsky Quartet, mezzo soprano Heather Shipp and jazz singer Jacqui Dankworth. You can download the e-flyer here.

Over the course of the year, Clare will give premieres of works for solo piano by Adam Gorb and Andrew Keeling, of works for trumpet and piano by John McCabe and Deborah Pritchard with trumpeter Simon Desbruslais, of songs by Robin Walker with soprano Jane Wilkinson, and of Urban Birds for three pianos, electronics and sampled birdsong by Arlene Sierra with pianists Xenia Pestova and Kathy Supove. The first of these premieres, of John McCabe's Sonata after William Byrd's 'Haec Dies', will take place on the 28 February at the Deal Festival in Kent.
