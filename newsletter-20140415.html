May is a busy month of new music as Clare gives three world premieres including Velocity by Adam Gorb, Coniunctio by Andrew Keeling and the Five Songs of Emily Brontë by Robin Walker. At the beginning of the month she will perform Prospero's Isle by James Francis Brown with cellist and narrator Matthew Sharp as part of RE:naissance at Kings Place. Designed to celebrate Shakespeare's legacy, you can find out more about this exciting festival by downloading a flyer here or listening to a podcast on Soundcloud.

Clare will then make her debut recitals at the Vale of Glamorgan and Beaumaris Festivals in Wales and returns to the Two Rivers Festival in the Wirral to perform with cellist Ken Woods, violinist Suzanne Casey, soprano Jane Wilkinson, and narrator Peter Davison in a Brontë-themed evening. At the end of the month she performs for the first time with Psappha, playing works by Peter Maxwell Davies, John Casken and Igor Stravinsky.

Clare has also recently been awarded a grant by the British Council's Artists' International Development Fund which will enable her to research Polish piano music and to tour Poland in the autumn. She will perform at the Chopin and his Europe Festival in August, lecture and play at a Panufnik centenary conference in Warsaw in September, and perform at Philharmonies across the country in October.
