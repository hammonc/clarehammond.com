Clare will give her debut recital at the Cheltenham Festival on Saturday 6 July with a programme inspired by Ancient Greece. A new piano cycle by Kenneth Hesketh, Horae (pro clara), will receive its world premiere, alongside music by Szymanowski and Satie. Clare will also participate in the festival's Composer Academy at a panel discussion to discuss the relationship between composer and performer. More information, including an audio excerpt from Horae, is available online.

You can read a review of Clare's recital at the Wigmore Hall in June on bachtrack and see a photograph of her just after the premiere of Robert's Saxton's Hortus Musicae for the City of London Festival the following week here. Highlights in August include collaborations with the Badke Quartet and violinist Sara Trickey, and a performance of Gabriel Jackson's piano concerto at the Presteigne Festival.


UPCOMING PERFORMANCES

  • 6 July: Satie, Szymanowski and Hesketh at the Cheltenham Festival
  • 20 July: Mozart and Brahms with the Goldfield Ensemble at the Hitchin Festival
  • 22-27 August: pianist-in-residence at the Presteigne Festival
  • 4-9 September: recording two discs for BIS Records at Potton Hall, Suffolk
