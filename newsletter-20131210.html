November has been a busy month with nine performances across the UK including a last-minute engagement at the Cadogan Hall when Clare stepped in to perform Bach's fifth Brandenburg Concerto and Vaughan Williams' Piano Quintet with the London International Players. She visited the Holywell Music Room in Oxford a few days later to perform works by Robert Saxton as part of his 60th birthday celebrations, described as a 'breath-taking' performance by the Oxford Magazine. Towards the end of the month, Clare gave the world premiere of Flight of the Killer Bee by Ewan Campbell, a humorous take on Rimsky-Korsakov's Flight of the Bumblebee, which she will be programming in spring 2014.

2013 has seen premieres of works by composers Ed Hughes, Robert Saxton, Ken Hesketh and Robert Peate at the Brighton, City of London, Cheltenham and Presteigne Festivals, four BBC radio broadcasts, and two CDs for BIS records which will be released in 2014 and 2015 respectively. Full details of past engagements are available on Clare's concerts archive. There are lots of exciting projects planned for 2014, of which more in January. In the meantime, have a very Merry Christmas and a Happy New Year!
