Clare's BBC recording of six etudes by Korean composer Unsuk Chin will be broadcast by BBC Radio 3 as part of Hear and Now on the evening of Saturday 5 January. Composed between 1995 and 2003, these fascinating studies are natural successors to the etudes by György Ligeti and are already of international prominence. With their scintillating textures and inventive use of timbre, they are absorbing and electrifying works which are sure to become a keystone of the piano repertoire. Tune in on the 5th or listen on BBC iPlayer until the 12th January.

In October Clare gave world premieres of two recently discovered pieces by Hamilton Harty as part of her inaugural recital as Artist-in-Residence at Queen's University Belfast. Spring Fancy and Portrait were composed by Harty in 1934 after he fell in love with a young woman, Lorie Bolland, while sailing to Australia. Listen to the full story from Professor Jan Smaczny who was interviewed with Clare by Marie-Louise Muir for BBC Radio Ulster here.

2013 is shaping up to be an exciting year with engagements at the Brighton Festival, the Wigmore Hall and the Presteigne Festival over the summer, and two solo recordings. Clare will also be premiering pieces written especially written for her by composers Robert Saxton and Kenneth Hesketh at the City of London Festival and the Cheltenham Festival. It would be lovely if you could join her at one of these events and, in the meantime, have a very Happy New Year!
