Clare's BBC recording of six etudes by Korean composer Unsuk Chin will be broadcast by BBC Radio 3 on Saturday 24 November at 10.10pm. Composed between 1995 and 2003, these fascinating studies are natural successors to the etudes by György Ligeti and are already of international prominence. With their scintillating textures and inventive use of timbre, they are absorbing and electrifying works which are sure to become a keystone of the piano repertoire. Tune in on Saturday evening or listen back on BBC iPlayer until 1 December.

In October Clare gave world premieres of two recently discovered pieces by Hamilton Harty as part of her inaugural recital as Artist-in-Residence at Queen's University Belfast. Spring Fancy and Portrait were composed by Harty in 1934 after he fell in love with a young woman, Lorie Bolland, while sailing to Australia. Listen to the full story from Professor Jan Smaczny who was interviewed with Clare by Marie-Louise Muir for BBC Radio Ulster here.
