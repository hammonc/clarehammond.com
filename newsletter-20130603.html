Clare will give two recitals in London in June, the first at the Wigmore Hall on Monday 17th, for the Park Lane Group, and the second for the City of London Festival on Monday 24th. The programme at the Wigmore Hall includes solo piano pieces by Bach, Schubert and Ginastera alongside piano trios by Haydn and Schumann, performed by the Lawson Trio.

Clare premieres a new work, Hortus Musicae, by Robert Saxton at the City of London Festival in a programme which touches on some the festival's themes for 2013: the environment and the musical life of Northern Ireland. A set of miniatures by Sibelius, The Trees, complements two pieces by Northern Irish composer Hamilton Harty, recently discovered after nearly 80 years and given their premiere by Clare in Belfast last October. Saxton's Chacony for left hand opens the programme, preceding Bach's Chaconne in D minor, transcribed for the left hand by Brahms.

  • 24 June: Saxton, Bach-Brahms, Harty, Sibelius at the City of London Festival

  • 20 July: Mozart and Brahms with the Goldfield Ensemble at the Hitchin Festival
