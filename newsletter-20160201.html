Clare is about to start a series of solo recitals across the UK with a Slavic programme of music including works by Chopin and Rachmaninov, and Mazurkas by Szymanowski and Thomas Adès. In February she performs for Music at Oxford, Lakeside Arts in Nottingham, in Keele and Hartlepool, and also plays concertos by Mozart and Chopin in Luton.

Clare then travels to Poland to tour concertos by Mozart and his Czech mentor, Josef Myslivecek. Myslivecek's first keyboard concerto has not been performed since his death in 1781 and is currently unpublished. Clare has created her own edition of the work and will give the 'modern-day world premiere' with the Kalisz Philharmonia and Adam Klocek, before taking it to Jelenia Gora and Lublin.

Clare was featured in the Evening Standard earlier in the week in connection with her performance in 'The Lady in the Van'. For those of you who missed the cinema release, the DVD is out on the 7 March!
