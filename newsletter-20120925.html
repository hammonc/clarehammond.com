Clare will make her first visit in October as Artist-in-Residence at Queen's University Belfast with a programme which includes world premieres of two pieces by the Northern Irish composer Hamilton Harty. Written nearly 80 years ago, Harty's 'Portrait' and 'Spring Fantasy' have only recently been discovered in a private collection in Chelsea. They will be premiered on Thursday 11 October alongside works by Handel, Szymanowski, Beethoven, Messiaen, Scriabin and Piers Hellawell's Piani, Latebre.

Before then, Clare will give her first concert with violinist Thomas Gould at Alleyn's School in Dulwich. Their programme will include works by Brahms, Franck and nocturnes by Chopin, arranged for violin and piano.

You can now find Clare on YouTube and can visit Clare's blog for tales of concerts past!
